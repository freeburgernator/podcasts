Its not a mirage, its an actual building - except the building doesn't exist. While he was telling me this, he said he could see skulls in the pavement. He also told me he had been raped by the police. He would not know what reality was.

How do you know what is real?

Have you ever realized that something you thought was true was not?

I don't advocate for open borders, but an expanse of desert is a literal trial by fire. Anyone who has been there can tell you that.

Anatomy of Doubt
The American Life

Soon after my father died, I began to make jokes about the agent of this death, a lasagna. That story is too long for this venue, but needless to say that some people take my humor as being in bad taste, as if my making light of the situation somehow means that I did not love or care for my father.

To those people, I would ask how they form that opinion. Is that what they have been taught? Who taught them that? What trama have they experienced that makes them an expert on how people experience trauma? And beyond that, what makes them an expert on how I process trauma?

What I am most shocked by is not just that the elderly women in this story must justify their doubt, but that they cannot say that they had no reason to doubt. Is their guilt so great that you must still worm your way out of it by blaming the victim? How would they know what they would do in the aftermath of such trauma?

My trauma was quite minor in comparison to this story of trauma - but I remember yearning for the world to return to normal. I heard that same yearn listening to this story, and perhaps that's why I take the doubters continued attempts at exoneration so damnable. At least Thomas would later die for Christ.

Have you ever doubted someone's trauma because of the way they acted?

When is it right to call someone a liar?
