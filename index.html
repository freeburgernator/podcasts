More things which we believe are reality are the results of other processes in our brain. This is our "hard wiring," and is probably impossible for us to change. Consider the colors you see: these were never taught to you, but something you have always known. They are hard wired. It is very fair to assume that we are under the influence of our own interpretations of reality, and those interpretations may be flawed. Based upon this understanding, we must doubt that we can be so quick to believe that the other person must see "reality" in the same way we do.

I recall sitting with a homeless man in Austin once. I had talked to him several times before. He admitted to me that he suffered from visual delusions. You should know that visual delusions are not like smudges that appear in your vision when you rube your eyes, but things that appear as real as everything else. Its not a mirage, its an actual building - except the building doesn't exist. While he was telling me this, he said he could see skulls in the pavement. He looked longingly at me and stated that he knew there weren't skulls there - he still had his logic with him, why would anyone put skulls in the pavenment - but he could not trust his eyes. He also told me he had been raped by the police. He would not know what reality was.

How do you know what is real?

Have you ever realized that something you thought was true was not?

Border Trilogy
Radiolab

I have had the chance of standing in the Sonoran and Chihuahuan deserts. They are hot and dry as hell. I luckily had the ability to get back into my car, drive with my license, and get waved through the checkpoints as I answered "yessir" to the "are you all US citizens?" in my American accent. I cannot imagine the desparation that drives people to walk through this desert - but I would say that if you are desparate enough to walk through that desert, you are no different than the select number of my ancestors who crossed the sea to get here - and if they are willing to cross that desert to get here, they are probably willing to do what it takes to be a profitable citizen for America. I don't advocate for open borders, but an expanse of desert is not an open door. Anyone who has been there can tell you that.

What would you cross a desert for?

What is a policy that is fair to all Americans and respects the rights of all people?
